What is Hacking?

Hacking is the process of identifying weaknesses in computer systems, networks, or software. These weaknesses can be used either to improve security or to cause harm. Not all hacking is bad, but illegal hacking causes serious problems worldwide.
Hacking affects governments, banks, schools, hospitals, and individuals. As Africa becomes more digital, cybersecurity awareness is very important.

Types of Hacking

White Hat Hacking

This is ethical hacking done legally to test and improve system security. White hat hackers help organizations protect data and prevent cyber attacks.
Black Hat Hacking

Black hat hacking is illegal and harmful. It includes stealing information, damaging systems, spreading malware, and committing cyber crimes.
Gray Hat Hacking

Gray hat hackers operate between legal and illegal activities. They may find security issues without permission but do not always intend harm.

Consequences of Hacking

Legal consequences such as fines, imprisonment, and criminal records.
Loss of personal privacy, identity theft, and financial loss.
Damage to businesses, government systems, and national security.
